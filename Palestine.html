The Devastating Consequences

1. Erasure of Palestinian Identity

The suppression of Palestinian people

Gaza has been home to Palestinians for generations. Forced relocation would sever their connection to the land, undermine their national identity, and effectively eliminate the possibility of a viable Palestinian state.

The psychological and cultural trauma of mass displacement would echo for generations, similar to the Nakba of 1948.

2. Destabilization of Egypt

Sinai is already a sensitive security area for Egypt. Introducing hundreds of thousands of displaced Palestinians would:

  • Strain Egypt's resources and infrastructure
  • Create new security challenges
  • Potentially violate Egypt's sovereignty
